Eliciting Probability Distributions with SHELF

Jeremy Oakley

2016-06-29

It is assumed that experts provide either probability or quantile judgements: the expert is either asked questions of the form, What is your probability $P(X<=10)$?'' or,Provide a value \(x\) such that you think \(P(X\le x)=0.5.\)’’ Either way, we think of the expert as providing points on his/her cumulative distribution function.

Expert 1 states \(P(X<30)=0.25, P(X<40)=0.5, P(X<50)=0.75\) Expert 2 states \(P(X<20)=0.25, P(X<25)=0.5, P(X<35)=0.75\) Both experts state \(0<X<100\)

The values of the quantiles are arranged in a matrix, with one column per expert

(v <- matrix(c(30, 40, 50, 20, 25, 35), 3, 2))
##      [,1] [,2]
## [1,]   30   20
## [2,]   40   25
## [3,]   50   35

(With one expert only, the values can be stored in a vector, as shown later.)

The probabilities should be arranged in matrix if they are different for the two experts, otherwise they are stored in a vector

library(SHELF)
(v <- matrix(c(30, 40, 50, 20, 25, 35), 3, 2))
##      [,1] [,2]
## [1,]   30   20
## [2,]   40   25
## [3,]   50   35
(p <- c(0.25, 0.5, 0.75))
## [1] 0.25 0.50 0.75
library(SHELF)
myfit <- fitdist(vals = v, probs = p, lower = 0, upper = 100)

Plot the fitted distributions, including a linear pool

plotfit(myfit, lp=T)

Now elicit a single ‘consensus’ distribution from the two experts Suppose they agree \(P(X<25)=0.25, P(X<30)=0.5, P(X<40)=0.75\)

v <-c(25, 30, 40)
p <-c(0.25, 0.5, 0.75)
myfit <- fitdist(vals = v, probs = p, lower = 0, upper = 100)

Plot the fitted density, and report some feedback, such as the fitted 5th and 95th percentiles

plotfit(myfit, ql=0.05, qu=0.95)

feedback(myfit, quantiles=c(0.05,0.95))
